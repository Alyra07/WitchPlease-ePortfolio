Witch, Please!

Witch, Please! is a shop simulation game set in a magical fantasy world. Players take on the role of a quirky young witch who recently opened her own enchantment workshop. Curious creatures visit the shop with unusual requests, from enchanted swords to cursed dolls. The core gameplay revolves around interacting with customers, enchanting their items based on their personalities and stories, and navigating light puzzle challenges on the enchanting workbench. The game blends storytelling, and fantasy-themed minigame mechanics in a cozy shop atmosphere.

    Key Features

  • A main shop window scene where customers arrive with their requests and give the player tasks to enchant their unique items
  • Dialogue system: Four distinct customers with unique items and stories
  • Minigames for 3-4 unique enchanting tools on the Workbench:
    • Cauldron: Brew a potion with correct ingredients
    • Cleaning Cloth: Drag this over the item to clean it
    • Magic Wand: Move your mouse cursor in the correct pattern
  • Task List UI: To make tracking customer requests easier
  • UI navigation between dialogue (Customer Window), and enchanting Workbench interactions
  • Visual storytelling through character design and shop atmosphere (Game Audio)
  • Main Menu Scene: Play / Exit game, Settings (Volume)

    Implemented Features (10.06.2025)

  • Scenes: MainMenu, CustomerWindow, Workbench
    • MainMenu: Start game, Exit game, Settings (Volume in Unity)
    • CustomerWindow: A customer walks to the counter, his item can be retrieved
    • Workbench: Where Minigames can be played for the current customer's item
  • GameStateManager: To handle persistent states needed in scenes (CustomerWindow & Workbench)
    • CustomerWindowState: Keeps track of the current customer and their item
    • WorkbenchState: Manages the state of the enchanting minigames
    • CustomerTaskList: A list of tasks (currently CauldronTask) can be applied to each customer
  • Cauldron Task: Brew potions with correct ingredients
    • 5 Ingredients: Drag & drop into the cauldron to brew a potion
    • 3 Potion Recipes: Players must put ingredients into the cauldron in the correct order
    • Potion Prefab: Is instantiated after correct brewing, and can be applied to the customer's item
  • Audio: Wwise project has been successfully integrated with Unity
  • Version Control: Unity VSC for collaboration

Story Outline

The player takes on the role of a silent witch who runs a small enchantment shop tucked away in a mysterious corner of the world. Throughout the game, four distinct characters visit the shop, each with a unique personality, magical request, and deeper motivations that slowly come to light. The gameplay revolves around interacting with these customers, enchanting their objects, and uncovering the threads that link their stories together. Though the witch never speaks, the world and its characters grow through these quiet encounters, building toward an unexpected climax.

Daran - The Wolf

Daran is a stoic, battle-hardened warrior. Cold, proud, and distant, he approaches the shop with a practical goal: to have his sword cleaned and re-enchanted. He treats the witch's shop as little more than a tool to serve his needs and barely acknowledges the witch beyond what's necessary. However, his return at the story's end reveals a deeper layer. Daran is not just a lone fighter - he's a protector, someone who's been watching from a distance. When danger arises from another customer's darker intentions, he steps back into the shop to intervene.

Finn - The Raccoon

Finn is an eccentric and curious traveler who stumbles into the shop entirely by accident. His energy is chaotic, his thoughts scattered, and his presence unpredictable. Despite his odd behavior, there's sincerity beneath the messiness. He brings a pouch for enchantment, claiming it holds his most important belongings - but it's clear there's something more personal and emotional attached to it. Finn's nervous energy masks a desire to protect something precious and private, hinting at vulnerability under his erratic charm.

Cael - The Bird

Cael is a quiet and thoughtful soul. Soft-spoken and hesitant, he visits the shop after hearing about it through Finn, though the two aren't close. Cael carries a single feather, a delicate object tied to a memory or a person from his past. He seeks enchantment not for protection or power, but for emotional closure. His request is tied to a piece of art he's trying to completeâ€”perhaps a poem, perhaps a farewell. His interaction with the witch is subtle but profound, reflecting a need to make peace with something unspoken.

Tara - The Cat

Tara is the final visitor, and the most dangerous. She arrives with charm and grace, cloaking her true intentions beneath a veil of flirtation and flattery. Her request - a simple enchantment on a doll - seems harmless at first. But it soon becomes clear that the doll is a vessel for a much darker spell, one designed to control or steal the witch's power. Tara's manipulation is clever and masked by charisma, but her plan is interrupted just in time by Daran, revealing a larger conflict at play.

System Design

Main Menu Scene

In the Main Menu, players can start the game (leads to Customer Window Scene) or exit. The scene will include a background image, the title, and buttons for game interactions, and adjusting settings (e.g. volume).

Customer Window Scene

In the Customer Window, players will interact with customers who arrive at the shop. Each customer has a unique item and story. Players can click on the item to retrieve it and start the enchanting process on the Workbench. Characters will arrive in a specific order, and players must complete their requests to return the item and move on to the next customer.

Workbench Scene

In the Workbench scene, players will perform enchanting minigames using various tools. Each tool has its own unique mechanics, such as dragging, clicking, or following patterns. Successfully enchanting the item will return the player to the Customer Window.
